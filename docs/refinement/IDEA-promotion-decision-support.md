# IDEA: Structured decision support at the IDEA promotion gate
**Created:** 2026-05-25
**Source:** Human structural review
**Status:** DRAFT
**Meta:** P0 | M | local | docs/refinement/

## Problem
Every IDEA promotion requires a human Decision field, but the human sees no structured comparison, no cost estimate, no dependency impact, no alternative evaluation at decision time. THINK generates rich analysis but delivers it ephemerally — terminal output only, not persisted to the decision surface. The bottleneck is not just throughput (see IDEA-idea-adjudication-throughput) — it is decision quality. A human adjudicating 33+ IDEAs with no structured support will make fast, low-quality decisions or avoid deciding entirely.

## Proposed solution
When a human opens an IDEA file to write a Decision, surface a structured decision brief alongside it: estimated implementation cost, open dependencies, similar past decisions and their outcomes, and THINK's most recent analysis (if any) persisted as a structured block in the IDEA file — not ephemeral terminal output. The brief should be generated by `arch analyze <IDEA-id>` and written into the IDEA's Gaps section before the human decides. The human still writes the Decision; the support removes the information deficit.

**External analysis (DeepSeek, 2026-05-25):** Proposes a 3-way forced choice (PROMOTE/REJECT/EXTEND) with auto-filled rationale templates alongside the decision brief. The brief is correct and should be adopted. The template mechanism is a risk: pre-rationalized options create the appearance of deliberation without its substance and could produce rubber-stamp decisions faster, not better. The brief surfaces information; the human constructs the rationale. Templates should not be adopted.

## Decision
EXTEND. Gap: the brief format output by `arch analyze <IDEA-id>` is undefined — no schema, no field list, no write target in the IDEA file. Without a concrete spec (max 5 fields, each with a named source), the implementation target is vague and risks building the wrong surface. Trigger: define the brief schema as a spec document (not a task — a one-page spec is sufficient). Templates for human rationale are explicitly excluded per the proposed solution. This must land before IDEA-idea-adjudication-throughput: decision quality before decision throughput.
